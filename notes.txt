# important points

PPL is still useful because it tells us whether a loss is good at a task (DTW is good at amplitude matching)

OOD experiments are avoided, despite their importance. Conducting OOD experiments is important for verification of loss function performance. 

Here we proposed a methodology to conduct OOD experiments (that is, experiments where the target cannot be reached) by designing synthesizers that partially overlap in functionality, and using the overlapping parameters as a partial form of parameter loss. 

We conduct out-of-domain experiments that measure loss function performance, and show the utility of PPL as a method of automatic evaluation and verification of loss function capabilities.
